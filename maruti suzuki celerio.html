Maruti suzuki celerio

Maruti Suzuki brought in the Celerio to replace two cars - the A-Star and the Estilo, and since its launch, the car has been clocking 1000 bookings a day.

This shows just how popular the Maruti Suzuki small car is.

Also Read: Maruti Suzuki Celerio Diesel Launched Maruti also brought it in with an Automated Manual Transmission, thus making Celerio the first car in the segment and price range to get it.

In fact, the difference between the one with the 5-speed manual transmission, and the one with the AMT was just 38000, thus making it the most affordable automatic in India.

Despite being based on the existing-generation A-Star, the Celerio is taller and more spacious than the former.

The car also shares the 1.0-litre K-Series engine with the A-Star that churns out a maximum power output of 67bhp, and returns an impressive mileage of 23.1Km/l.
